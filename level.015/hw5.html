Guria is a region in western Georgia known for its diverse nature, including mountainous areas and a Black Sea coastline. The region is rich in mineral waters, such as those found in the Nabeghlavi spring, and is a popular destination for health resorts like Ureki and Shekvetili, which feature magnetic sand. Guria's administrative center is Ozurgeti, and it is bordered by the Black Sea to the west and the Adjara-Guria Ridge to the south.
